🧭 Real Workflow Example
  1. Search patient
  2. If not found → Add patient
  3. Register patient (Check-In)
  4. Click Activate
  5. Doctor consults / services added
  6. Add Order (items)
  7. System creates invoice automatically
  8. Take payment OR use deposit
  9. Print receipt

📝 Start Visit (Check-In)
  • Select patient
  • Fill registration form
  • Click Submit
🔥 CRITICAL STEP: ACTIVATE

After submitting, you MUST click Activate.

  • ❌ No Activate → No billing
  • ❌ No Activate → Orders will NOT charge
  • ❌ No Activate → Invoice will NOT work
  • ✅ Activate = patient ready for services
🧠 Why Activate Matters

Activate tells the system that the visit is officially started. Without it, the system blocks all billing actions.

📊 Status Meaning
  • Draft → Not submitted
  • Pending → Submitted but not activated
  • ACTIVE → Ready for billing & services
  • Completed → Visit finished
✅ Complete Visit
  • Mark visit as completed after services
  • Ensures visit is properly closed
  • Prevents further unintended changes
❌ Common Mistakes
  • Submitting without activating
  • Trying to bill in Pending status
  • Forgetting to complete visit

🧾 Services / Orders
  • Click Add Order
  • Select service / item
  • Enter quantity
  • Save
⚙️ Billing Trigger (IMPORTANT)

Adding items in an order is what creates charges.

  • ✅ Item added → Charge created
  • ✅ Charges → Invoice generated
  • ❌ No item → No invoice
⚙️ Automatic Billing (System Controlled)
  • Some charges are added automatically
  • Based on visit type or services
  • System ensures correct pricing

❌ Do not expect all charges to be manual

🧠 How Billing Works
  1. Add service (item)
  2. System calculates price automatically
  3. Invoice is created or updated
  4. Patient balance increases
📄 Orders → Invoice

You do NOT manually create invoices. The system creates invoices automatically from orders.

📦 Billable Items (Source of Charges)
  • All charges come from billable items
  • Items have predefined prices
  • Orders use these items to create invoices

❌ No item configured → Cannot bill service

⚠️ Key Rule

No item = No bill

❌ Common Mistakes
  • Adding order without items
  • Expecting invoice before adding services
  • Forgetting quantity

Invoice Exists
Choose Payment

💳 Payment
  • Select patient
  • Select invoice
  • Enter amount
  • Save payment
⚙️ Payment Logic
  • ✅ Payment reduces invoice balance
  • ❌ Cannot pay without invoice
  • ❌ Cannot pay more than balance
⚖️ Payment vs Deposit (VERY IMPORTANT)
  • Payment → Pays an invoice
  • Deposit → Stores money for later

❌ Deposit does NOT reduce invoice automatically

✅ Deposit must be applied when paying invoice

🧭 When To Use What
  • After service → Payment
  • Before service → Deposit
  • No invoice → Cannot take payment
🏦 Deposit

Deposit is extra money stored for the patient. It can be used later to pay invoices.

↩️ Refunds
  • Returns money from a payment
  • Reduces total collected amount
  • Linked to an existing payment
↩️ Deposit Refund
  • Returns unused deposit
  • Does NOT affect invoice balance
  • Reduces patient stored deposit
❌ Common Mistakes
  • Using deposit instead of payment
  • Expecting deposit to auto-pay invoice
  • Overpaying invoice

🟣 Insurance System

Insurance allows patients to receive services without paying immediately. Claims are later reviewed and paid by insurers.

👤 Patient Insurance
  • Assign insurance to patient
  • Enter policy details
  • Ensure coverage is active
📄 Insurance Claims
  • Claims are created from invoices
  • Only insurance patients generate claims
  • Tracks what insurance owes
📊 Claim Status
  • DRAFT → Not submitted
  • SUBMITTED → Sent
  • APPROVED → Accepted
  • PARTIAL → Part accepted
  • REJECTED → Denied
  • PAID → Completed
⚠️ Rules
  • Insurance works AFTER invoice
  • No invoice → No claim
  • Approval ≠ Payment
❌ Common Mistakes
  • Expecting instant payment
  • Creating claim without invoice
  • Confusing approval with payment
